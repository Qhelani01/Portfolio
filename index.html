Through My Lens

the "I want to go there."

type of images

From Pacific coastlines to African skies, these are places that got under my skin. Follow @qhelani_moyo for more, and read the full stories on my photography blog.

California

The West Coast has a way of slowing time. Fog on the Golden Gate, golden light on the Pacific, and quiet moments in Point Reyes—where a quail might cross your path and remind you why you came. These are the frames I keep coming back to.

Seattle

Seattle at golden hour feels like the city is holding its breath. The light rolls over the skyline and turns everything soft. I had to stop and just look—and then pick up the camera.

Victoria Falls & Zimbabwe

Victoria Falls doesn’t feel real until you’re there. The spray, the rainbows, the Zambezi rolling off the edge—and then an elephant walking down the road like it owns the place. In the same trip, tiny weavers building nests in the trees. It gets into your bones.

Botswana

Safari in Chobe is one of those experiences you can’t really explain—you have to stand in the landscape and feel it. The light, the space, and the wildlife moving through it. I’ve never met anyone who regretted going.
